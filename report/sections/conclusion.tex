\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This report formulated operating-theatre planning as a stochastic program solved
through sample average approximation, progressing from operational scheduling
(Steps~1--3: timing, sequencing, and full assignment) and evaluation
of these models (Step~4: SAA convergence, EVPI, and VSS) to a tactical three-stage
blueprint that fixes a per-session case-mix template before the waiting list is
known (Step~5).

At the operational level
(Section~\ref{subsec:operational_insights}), waiting time dominates cost, so good
schedules build in early slack and trade a little idle time to prevent overrun
cascades; specialty mismatches arise only under a capacity shortage, and the positive VSS with a large EVPI shows that
explicitly modelling duration uncertainty, rather than planning against mean
durations, yields better performing schedules. At the
tactical level (Section~\ref{sec:bp_results}), the blueprint can offer a robust interpretable solution
across different realizations but does constrain the operational planning such that ultimately it will negatively affect the schedule quality. 
Imposing the template did on the evaluated test instances however yield relatively good performance.
However, as the blueprint is more tight, the operational schedule is more restricted by the blueprint such that feasibility of scheduling all patients is no longer guaranteed.

The two levels are complementary: the blueprint structures which cases may
meet which session, while the operational model schedules optimally within these restrictions.
